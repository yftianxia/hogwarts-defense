## 1. Auto-Attack vs Spells

### Correction to Prompt Assumption
The prompt states "Apprentice (60 HP): 3 auto-attack shots = ~1s." This math is incorrect.
3 shots * 7 damage = 21 damage, which is 35% of an apprentice's 60 HP. The actual
shots-to-kill for an apprentice is **9 shots** (8.57, rounded up), taking **~3.15 seconds**
of uninterrupted auto-attack. The user likely confused Q-assisted kill time with pure
auto-attack time. With Q (45) + 3 auto-attack shots (21) = 66, an apprentice dies
in ~1.4 seconds — but that requires Q, not auto-attack alone.

### DPS Comparison

| Source | Sustained DPS (single target) | Burst | AoE |
|--------|------|-------|-----|
| Auto-attack | 20.0 | 7 per 0.35s | No |
| Q | 15.0 | 45 instant | Yes (200px) |
| R | 5.5 (max) | 66 over 3s | Yes (small) |
| E | 0 | 0 | CC only |
| F | 0 | 0 | CC only |

**Finding: Auto-attack is the dominant damage source.** Auto-attack (20 DPS)
out-performs Q on single targets (15 sustained DPS). Q's value comes from:
- AoE (multiplies against grouped enemies: 45 * N)
- Knockback (survival utility, not captured in DPS)
- Burst (front-loaded damage kills enemies before they reach the gate)

R contributes only 5.5 sustained single-target DPS — the lowest damage source.
Since R is placed at the nearest enemy's position at cast time and does not follow
them, real-world damage is often less than the theoretical 66 (enemies exit the fire).

### Verdict: SPELLS ARE REASONABLY BALANCED BUT R IS WEAK

Auto-attack being the primary damage source is acceptable design — it makes
positioning matter and rewards consistent play. Q's AoE burst gives it a clear
role (wave clear). E and F are pure utility (control).

**R is the outlier.** For a 12-second cooldown ability labeled as an "ultimate,"
66 theoretical damage (5.5 sustained DPS) is underwhelming. In practice, enemies
often take only 1-2 seconds of fire (~22-44 damage) before moving past it.

**Recommendation:** Increase R damage from 22/s to 28-30/s, OR increase duration
from 3s to 4s, OR make the fire wall wider (increase halfWidth from 45 to 60).
Any single change would improve R's impact without breaking balance.

No enemy type is either trivially one-shot by auto-attack or excessively spongy.

## 3. Player Survival

### Time-to-Die (Harry has 100 HP, no recovery)

| Threat | DPS vs Harry | Time to Kill Harry |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| 1 Apprentice (contact only) | 8/s | 12.5s |
| 1 Elite (contact only) | 15/s | 6.7s |
| 1 Boss (contact only) | 25/s | 4.0s |
| 2 Elites (contact only) | 30/s | 3.3s |
| 4 Elites (contact, worst case W3) | 60/s | 1.7s |
| Apprentice projectile | ~1.6 DPS each | — |
| Elite projectile | ~3.0 DPS each | — |
| Boss projectile | ~5.0 DPS | — |

### Key Finding: NO HEALTH RECOVERY

Harry has 100 HP with no regeneration, no healing pickups, and no recovery
between waves. Across 41 total enemies (30 apprentices, 10 elites, 1 boss),
every point of damage is permanent.

**Chip damage accumulation scenario:**
- Wave 1: 2-3 apprentice projectiles hit = ~8-12 HP lost
- Wave 2: 2-3 elite projectiles hit = ~15-22 HP lost
- Wave 3: 3-4 elite projectiles hit = ~22-30 HP lost
- Entering wave 4: potentially at 36-55 HP
- Boss projectile (12.5 dmg) + contact = very dangerous

### Mitigating Factors
- Harry's speed (200 px/s) vastly outpaces all enemies (35-75 px/s) — **kiting is extremely effective**
- Enemy projectiles travel at 200 px/s — equal to Harry's speed, dodgeable by strafing
- Q knockback (80px) creates distance on demand
- E stun (1.5s, AoE cone) interrupts attack cycles
- F freeze (3s, single target) disables the biggest threat
- Enemies path toward the gate first, then toward Harry — time to thin them out

### Verdict: CHALLENGING BUT FAIR — FLAG FOR PLAYTEST

The survival balance rewards skillful kiting and ability usage. A player who
dodges projectiles and uses CC effectively can clear the level with minimal
damage taken. A player who face-tanks will die quickly.

**Concern for new players:** The lack of any recovery mechanic means early
mistakes compound. A player eating 30-40 chip damage in waves 1-2 enters
wave 3 at a significant disadvantage with no way to recover.

**Recommendation (advisory, not blocking):** Consider adding one of:
- 15 HP restored between waves
- Small HP drops from elite kills (5-8 HP)
- Or keep as-is but document that Level 1 intentionally has no recovery
  to teach dodging — this is a valid design choice

## 4. Wave Pacing

### Spawn Pressure Analysis

| Wave | Total HP Pool | Spawn Rate | HP Influx/sec | Est. Clear Time |
|------|--------------|------------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | 720 (12 * 60) | 1.0s | 60 HP/s | 20-24s |
| 2 | 1200 (10*60 + 4*150) | 0.8s | 150 HP/s (avg) | 23-29s |
| 3 | 1380 (8*60 + 6*150) | 0.6s | 230 HP/s (avg) | 23-33s |
| 4 | 500 (1 * 500) | 2.0s | 250 HP/s (burst) | 15-25s |

### Difficulty Curve Assessment

The wave design creates a clean escalating difficulty curve:
- **Wave 1:** Tutorial wave — 12 slow apprentices, easy kiting practice
- **Wave 2:** Introduces elites — first real threat, teaches priority targeting
- **Wave 3:** Peak pressure — 6 elites at 0.6s spawns, tests all skills
- **Wave 4:** Boss climax — single high-HP threat, tests endurance and single-target DPS

The pause duration (3s) is brief but sufficient — it gives the player a moment
to reposition without killing momentum.

**Concern: Wave 3 spawn density.** At 0.6s intervals with 6 elites in the mix,
the HP influx rate (230 HP/s average) exceeds even perfect single-target DPS
(~40 DPS). The player MUST use Q AoE and R area denial to keep up. This is
appropriate difficulty for the penultimate wave but may overwhelm new players.

### Verdict: GOOD PACING — WAVE 3 IS THE REAL TEST

The 1.5-2 minute total duration is appropriate for a first level. The
sawtooth difficulty pattern (build, release, build higher, boss climax)
follows established flow state design.
